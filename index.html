I am a third-year CS PhD student at University of Wisconsin–Madison, supervised by Yin Li. I am grateful to have been mentored by Pedro Morgado at Skild AI, Yuheng Li and Jiuxiang Gu at Adobe Research.

Previously, I graduated from the MSCV program at Carnegie Mellon University, supervised by Fernando de la Torre and Shayok Chakraborty. I received my B.S. in CS and Math from Hong Kong University of Science and Technology with First Class Honors, supervised by Chi-Keung Tang and Yu-Wing Tai.

Research Interests

I am interested in building multi-modal generative models, including:

  • Controllability: generating visual content that precisely follows multi-modal conditions such as text and audio — e.g., animating images in sync with sound (ASVA)
  • Training & scalability: designing efficient training recipes that scale generative models to larger sizes, more data, and new capabilities (OpenASVA, UniTemp)
  • Efficiency: reducing the memory and compute cost of generative models at both training and inference time, towards fast few-step generation (UniTemp)
